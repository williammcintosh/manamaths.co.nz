{\LARGE \textbf{L02 Equivalent fractions}}\\[0.35em]
Find or identify fractions that are equivalent.

\vspace{2.6em}
{\LARGE \textbf{Excellence}}
\begin{multicols}{3}
\begin{enumerate}[
  itemsep=7.4em,
  label=\textbf{\arabic*.}, 
  labelwidth=2.2em,
  labelsep=0.75em,
  leftmargin=!,
  align=left
]
\item Explain whether \textbf{14/21} and \textbf{2/3} are equivalent.
\item Explain whether \textbf{18/24} and \textbf{3/5} are equivalent.
\item One fraction is \textbf{3/4}. Write three different equivalent fractions.
\item Fill in both blanks: \rule{1.2cm}{0.15mm}\textbf{/12 = 3/4 = 21/}\rule{1.2cm}{0.15mm}
\item A student says \textbf{4/6} and \textbf{8/10} are equivalent because both numbers doubled. Are they correct?
\item Shade a diagram to show a fraction equivalent to \textbf{2/5}.
\item Complete: if $\textbf{a/b} = \textbf{12/20}$, then a simpler equivalent fraction is \rule{2cm}{0.15mm}.
\item Which does not belong: \textbf{1/2}, \textbf{2/4}, \textbf{3/6}, \textbf{4/10}?
\item Write a real-life example that could be represented by two equivalent fractions.
\item Write your own pair of equivalent fractions and explain how you know.
\end{enumerate}
\end{multicols}

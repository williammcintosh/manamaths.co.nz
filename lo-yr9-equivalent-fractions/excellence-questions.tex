\task Explain whether \mbox{$\frac{14}{21}$} and \mbox{$\frac{2}{3}$} are equivalent.

\task One fraction is \mbox{$\frac{3}{4}$}. Write three different equivalent fractions.
\task Fill in both blanks: \mbox{$\frac{\square}{12} = \frac{3}{4} = \frac{21}{\square}$}.
\task A student says \mbox{$\frac{4}{6}$} and \mbox{$\frac{8}{10}$} are equivalent because both numbers doubled. Are they correct?
\task Shade a diagram to show a fraction equivalent to \mbox{$\frac{2}{5}$}.
\task Complete: if \mbox{$\frac{a}{b} = \frac{12}{20}$}, then a simpler equivalent fraction is \rule{2cm}{0.15mm}.
\task Which does not belong: \mbox{$\frac{1}{2}$}, \mbox{$\frac{2}{4}$}, \mbox{$\frac{3}{6}$}, \mbox{$\frac{4}{10}$}?

\task Write your own pair of equivalent fractions and explain how you know.
\task Explain whether \mbox{$\frac{15}{20}$} and \mbox{$\frac{9}{12}$} are equivalent.
\task Fill in both blanks: \mbox{$\frac{\square}{18} = \frac{2}{3} = \frac{16}{\square}$}.

\task Complete: if \mbox{$\frac{x}{y} = \frac{15}{25}$}, then a simpler equivalent fraction is \rule{2cm}{0.15mm}.

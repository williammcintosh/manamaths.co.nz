{\LARGE \textbf{L02 Equivalent fractions}}\\[0.35em]
Find or identify fractions that are equivalent.

\vspace{2.6em}
{\LARGE \textbf{Excellence}}
\begin{multicols}{3}
\begin{enumerate}[
  itemsep=8.8em,
  label=\textbf{\arabic*.}, 
  labelwidth=2.2em,
  labelsep=0.75em,
  leftmargin=!,
  align=left
]
\item Explain whether $\frac{14}{21}$ and $\frac{2}{3}$ are equivalent.
\item Explain whether $\frac{18}{24}$ and $\frac{3}{5}$ are equivalent.
\item One fraction is $\frac{3}{4}$. Write three different equivalent fractions.
\item Fill in both blanks: $\frac{\square}{12} = \frac{3}{4} = \frac{21}{\square}$
\item A student says $\frac{4}{6}$ and $\frac{8}{10}$ are equivalent because both numbers doubled. Are they correct?
\item Shade a diagram to show a fraction equivalent to $\frac{2}{5}$.
\item Complete: if $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{12}{20}$, then a simpler equivalent fraction is \rule{2cm}{0.15mm}.
\item Which does not belong: $\frac{1}{2}$, $\frac{2}{4}$, $\frac{3}{6}$, $\frac{4}{10}$?
\item Write a real-life example that could be represented by two equivalent fractions.
\item Write your own pair of equivalent fractions and explain how you know.
\end{enumerate}
\end{multicols}
